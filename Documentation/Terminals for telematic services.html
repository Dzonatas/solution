T.200

[Withdrawn] Programmable communication interface for terminal equipment connected to ISDN  
This Recommendation had been approved at the WTSC 1996. In its Appendix, it describes one of the programmable communication interfaces which were available on the market at that time. Although such an interface does not affect the communication between networks but is rather a matter for implementation at a local computer (e.g. PC), the related study Question had been accepted at the WTSC-96 to promote computer communication via the ISDN, and thus the growth of the ISDNs. After 1996,the quick market development made further studies in the ITU-T obsolete. ITU-T came to the conclusion that it would be appropriate to delete T.200 in order to avoid any further maintenance work which might bind resources unnecessarily
